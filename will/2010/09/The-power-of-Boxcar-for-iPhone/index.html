The Power Of Boxcar For iPhone

by @im_a_muppet on September 15, 2010

Boxcar is an app and service that pushes data to your iPhone. You can push updates form Twitter, Facebook, GMail… Download the app here (it is free but ad supported) and connect to the services you want pushed to you. It even opens the up the Twitter and Facebook app when you view a notification.

So why would you want to use Boxcar for apps that have native push (Facebook, Mail and soon Twitter)? I am going to list a few reasons and give real examples based on my own setup.

1. Quiet Time

Although the native apps may have push with Boxcar you can setup quiet time. Basically this is time when notifications will not be pushed to you. I have mine set as follows

Currently this is all or nothing though. At present you cannot have different quiet times for different services.

2. More Sound options

There are many different sounds that you can use for incoming notifications. I have different ones for email (which is more important) than for Twitter and Facebook.

And if you have different setups for the same account as is explained in the next section you can have different sounds for DMs in Twitter than you do for @mentions.

3. Multiple setups for one account

You can also create multiple connections to the one account. For example I have multiple connections to Twitter. I have one for the important stuff (DMs and @mentions) and another for a list I created of people I want update from (I could use my timeline too but that is too busy).

With this setup I get all the important ones and a subset of my timeline pushed to me. I could also use a different sound for each so I know what was just pushed.

Similarly you may want to setup a filter in GMail for Important mail and have that sent to push to you separately than your regular inbox. That way you can turn off all services in an meetings for example but be use to get important messages pushed to you.

4. Send whatever you want via Boxcar’s API

Boxcar also has an API for you to play with so that you get what ever notifications you desire.
